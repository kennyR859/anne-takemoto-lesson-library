Part 1 — Logarithms
Problem 1
Use change-of-base to evaluate \(\log_5(28)\). Exact + 4 decimals.
Change of Base Log Evaluation
Step 1. Apply change-of-base formula: \(\log_b(a) = \dfrac{\ln a}{\ln b}\).
Step 2. Exact: \(\log_5(28) = \dfrac{\ln 28}{\ln 5} = \dfrac{\log 28}{\log 5}\).
Step 3. Decimal: \(\dfrac{3.33220}{1.60944} \approx 2.07039\).
\(\log_5(28) = \dfrac{\ln 28}{\ln 5} \approx 2.0704\)
Problem 2
Expand \(\log_3\!\left(\dfrac{x^{2}\sqrt{y}}{z^{4}}\right)\).
Quotient Rule Product Rule Power Rule
Step 1. Quotient rule: \(\log_3(x^2\sqrt{y}) - \log_3(z^4)\).
Step 2. Product rule on numerator: \(\log_3(x^2) + \log_3(\sqrt{y}) - \log_3(z^4)\).
Step 3. Rewrite radical: \(\sqrt{y} = y^{1/2}\). Power rule on each term: \[ 2\log_3(x) + \tfrac{1}{2}\log_3(y) - 4\log_3(z). \]
\(2\log_3(x) + \tfrac{1}{2}\log_3(y) - 4\log_3(z)\)
Problem 3
Condense \(3\ln(x) - \tfrac{1}{2}\ln(y+1) + 2\ln(z)\).
Power Rule (reverse) Combine into Single Log
Step 1. Reverse power rule: \(\ln(x^3) - \ln\!\bigl((y+1)^{1/2}\bigr) + \ln(z^2)\).
Step 2. Combine sum: \(\ln(x^3 z^2) - \ln(\sqrt{y+1})\).
Step 3. Combine difference into quotient.
\(\ln\!\left(\dfrac{x^{3}\,z^{2}}{\sqrt{y+1}}\right)\)
Problem 4
Solve \(\log_2(x-3) + \log_2(x+1) = 5\). State domain; reject extraneous.
Log Equation Domain Restriction Extraneous Check
Step 1 (Domain). Need \(x - 3 > 0\) AND \(x + 1 > 0\) \(\Rightarrow x > 3\).
Step 2. Combine logs: \(\log_2\!\bigl((x-3)(x+1)\bigr) = 5\).
Step 3. Convert to exponential form: \((x-3)(x+1) = 2^5 = 32\).
Step 4. Expand: \(x^2 - 2x - 3 = 32 \Rightarrow x^2 - 2x - 35 = 0\).
Step 5. Factor: \((x-7)(x+5) = 0 \Rightarrow x = 7\) or \(x = -5\).
Step 6 (Reject). \(x = -5\) violates \(x > 3\) (extraneous). Keep \(x = 7\).
\(x = 7\)
Part 2 — Exponential Modeling
Problem 5
\(\$4{,}500\) at \(4.2\%\) continuously compounded. When reaches \(\$10{,}000\)?
Continuous Compound Interest \(A = Pe^{rt}\)
Step 1. Set up: \(10{,}000 = 4{,}500\,e^{0.042\,t}\).
Step 2. Isolate exponential: \(\dfrac{10{,}000}{4{,}500} = \dfrac{20}{9} = e^{0.042\,t}\).
Step 3. Take natural log: \(\ln\!\left(\dfrac{20}{9}\right) = 0.042\,t\).
Step 4. Solve: \(t = \dfrac{\ln(20/9)}{0.042} = \dfrac{0.79851}{0.042} \approx 19.012\).
\(t \approx 19.01\) years
Problem 6
\(A(t) = A_0 e^{kt}\), half-life 28 yr. (a) Find \(k\). (b) Time until \(12\%\) remains.
Half-Life Exponential Decay \(\ln\) Manipulation
Part (a) Step 1. At \(t = 28\), \(A = \tfrac{1}{2}A_0\): \(\;\tfrac{1}{2}A_0 = A_0 e^{28k}\).
Step 2. Cancel \(A_0\): \(\tfrac{1}{2} = e^{28k}\).
Step 3. Take \(\ln\): \(\ln\!\bigl(\tfrac{1}{2}\bigr) = 28k \Rightarrow k = -\dfrac{\ln 2}{28}\).
Decimal: \(k \approx -0.02476\) per year.
Part (b) Step 1. Set \(0.12\,A_0 = A_0 e^{kt}\) \(\Rightarrow 0.12 = e^{kt}\).
Step 2. Take \(\ln\): \(\ln(0.12) = kt\).
Step 3. Substitute \(k\): \(t = \dfrac{\ln(0.12)}{-\ln(2)/28} = -\dfrac{28\,\ln(0.12)}{\ln(2)}\).
Step 4. Decimal: \(t = -\dfrac{28(-2.1203)}{0.6931} \approx \dfrac{59.37}{0.6931} \approx 85.66\).
(a) \(k = -\dfrac{\ln 2}{28} \approx -0.0248\)    (b) \(t \approx 85.66\) years
Problem 7
Rewrite \(f(t) = 600(1.035)^t\) as \(600 e^{rt}\). Find \(r\) and interpret.
Base Conversion \(\ln\) Identity
Step 1. Equate exponentials: \((1.035)^t = e^{rt}\).
Step 2. Use identity \(a^t = e^{t\ln a}\): \(\;e^{t\ln(1.035)} = e^{rt}\).
Step 3. Match exponents: \(r = \ln(1.035)\).
Step 4. Decimal: \(r \approx 0.03440\), so the continuous growth rate is \(\approx 3.44\%\) per unit of \(t\) — slightly less than the discrete \(3.5\%\), since continuous growth compounds infinitely often.
\(r = \ln(1.035) \approx 0.0344\)  ⇒  \(\approx 3.44\%\) continuous
Part 3 — Fundamental Theorem of Algebra
Problem 8
\((2-i)\) is a zero of \(P(x) = x^4 - 5x^3 + 7x^2 + 3x - 10\). Find all zeros and write linear factored form.
Conjugate Root Theorem Polynomial Long Division
Step 1. Real coefficients \(\Rightarrow\) complex zeros come in conjugate pairs. So \((2 + i)\) is also a zero.
Step 2. Build the quadratic factor: \[ \bigl(x-(2-i)\bigr)\bigl(x-(2+i)\bigr) = x^2 - 4x + (4 - i^2) = x^2 - 4x + 5. \]
Step 3. Divide \(P(x)\) by \(x^2 - 4x + 5\): \[ \dfrac{x^4 - 5x^3 + 7x^2 + 3x - 10}{x^2 - 4x + 5} = x^2 - x - 2. \]
Step 4. Factor remaining quadratic: \(x^2 - x - 2 = (x-2)(x+1)\).
Step 5. All zeros: \(x = 2,\;\; -1,\;\; 2-i,\;\; 2+i\).
\(P(x) = (x-2)(x+1)\bigl(x-(2-i)\bigr)\bigl(x-(2+i)\bigr)\)
Problem 9
Degree 4, real integer coefficients. Zeros: \(x = 3\) (mult. 2), \(x = 1+2i\). Standard form.
Conjugate Root Theorem Polynomial Construction
Step 1. Real coefs \(\Rightarrow\) \(1 - 2i\) is also a zero. Four zeros: \(3, 3, 1+2i, 1-2i\).
Step 2. Build: \[ P(x) = (x-3)^{2}\bigl(x-(1+2i)\bigr)\bigl(x-(1-2i)\bigr). \]
Step 3. Multiply complex pair: \[ \bigl(x-(1+2i)\bigr)\bigl(x-(1-2i)\bigr) = (x-1)^2 - (2i)^2 = (x-1)^2 + 4 = x^2 - 2x + 5. \]
Step 4. Multiply \((x-3)^2 = x^2 - 6x + 9\) with \(x^2 - 2x + 5\): \[ (x^2 - 6x + 9)(x^2 - 2x + 5) = x^4 - 8x^3 + 26x^2 - 48x + 45. \]
\(P(x) = x^{4} - 8x^{3} + 26x^{2} - 48x + 45\)
Part 4 — Synthetic Division & Factor Theorem
Problem 10
Synthetic division: \((3x^4 - 8x^3 + 5x - 7) \div (x - 2)\). Verify with Remainder Theorem.
Synthetic Division Remainder Theorem
Step 1. Coefficients (insert 0 for missing \(x^2\)): \(\;3,\; -8,\; 0,\; 5,\; -7\). Divisor root: \(c = 2\).
Step 2. Synthetic division table:
\(\begin{array}{r|rrrrr} 2 & 3 & -8 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ & & 6 & -4 & -8 & -6 \\ \hline & 3 & -2 & -4 & -3 & \boxed{-13} \end{array}\)
Step 3. Read off: quotient \(Q(x) = 3x^3 - 2x^2 - 4x - 3\), remainder \(R = -13\).
Step 4 (Verify). By Remainder Theorem, \(P(2)\) should equal \(R\): \[ P(2) = 3(16) - 8(8) + 0 + 5(2) - 7 = 48 - 64 + 10 - 7 = -13. \;\checkmark \]
\(Q(x) = 3x^{3} - 2x^{2} - 4x - 3,\quad R = -13\)
Problem 11
Find \(k\) so \((x+4)\) is a factor of \(P(x) = 2x^3 + kx^2 - 11x - 12\).
Factor Theorem Solve for Parameter
Step 1 (Factor Theorem). \((x+4)\) is a factor \(\iff P(-4) = 0\).
Step 2. Substitute \(x = -4\): \[ P(-4) = 2(-4)^3 + k(-4)^2 - 11(-4) - 12 = -128 + 16k + 44 - 12. \]
Step 3. Simplify: \(P(-4) = 16k - 96\).
Step 4. Set to zero: \(16k - 96 = 0 \Rightarrow k = 6\).
\(k = 6\)
Part 5 — Polynomial Graphing
Problem 12
\(P(x) = -(x+1)^2 (x-2)^3 (x-4)\). Degree, end behavior, zeros & multiplicity, \(y\)-intercept, sketch.
Multiplicity Bounce / Cross / Slide End Behavior
(a) Degree & End Behavior: Sum of multiplicities \(= 2 + 3 + 1 = 6\). Leading term comes from \(-x^2 \cdot x^3 \cdot x = -x^6\). Degree even & leading coefficient negative \(\Rightarrow\) both ends point down. \[ \text{As } x \to \pm\infty,\; P(x) \to -\infty. \quad \downarrow \;\downarrow \]
(b) Zeros & Multiplicity:
  • \(x = -1\), multiplicity \(2\) (even) \(\rightarrow\) bounce
  • \(x = 2\), multiplicity \(3\) (odd, \(>1\)) \(\rightarrow\) slide / flat-cross
  • \(x = 4\), multiplicity \(1\) \(\rightarrow\) cross (linear)
(c) \(y\)-intercept: \(P(0) = -(1)^2(-2)^3(-4) = -(1)(-8)(-4) = -32.\) Point: \((0,\,-32)\).
(d) Sketch summary (sign chart):
Interval \((-\infty,\,-1)\) \((-1,\,2)\) \((2,\,4)\) \((4,\,\infty)\)
Sign of \(P\) \(-\) \(-\) \(+\) \(-\)
Curve: comes up from \(-\infty\) on the left → touches and bounces at \(x=-1\) (stays below) → passes through \((0,-32)\) → flat-crosses through \(x=2\) (rises above axis) → peaks → crosses at \(x=4\) (drops back below) → \(-\infty\) on the right.
Degree 6, ends \(\downarrow\downarrow\); zeros: \(-1\) (bounce), \(2\) (slide), \(4\) (cross); \(y\)-int \((0, -32)\)
