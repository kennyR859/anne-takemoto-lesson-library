Part 1 — Logarithm Properties
1. \(\log_3(81)\)
\(3^{4} = 81 \Rightarrow\) 4
2. \(\log_2(1/16)\)
\(2^{-4} = 1/16 \Rightarrow\) \(-4\)
3. \(\log_5(125)\)
\(5^{3} = 125 \Rightarrow\) 3
4. \(\ln(e^{7})\)
\(\ln(e^x) = x \Rightarrow\) 7
5. \(\log(10{,}000)\)
\(10^{4} = 10{,}000 \Rightarrow\) 4
6. \(\log_4(8) + \log_4(2)\)
\(=\log_4(16) = 2\). 2
7. \(\log_3(54) - \log_3(2)\)
\(=\log_3(27) = 3\). 3
8. Expand \(\log_2(x^{2}\,y^{3})\)
Product rule then power rule: \(\log_2(x^2) + \log_2(y^3) = 2\log_2(x) + 3\log_2(y)\).
\(2\log_2(x) + 3\log_2(y)\)
9. Expand \(\ln\!\left(\sqrt{a/b}\right)\)
Power: \(\ln(a/b)^{1/2} = \tfrac{1}{2}\ln(a/b)\).
Quotient: \(\tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(\ln a - \ln b\bigr)\).
\(\tfrac{1}{2}\ln(a) - \tfrac{1}{2}\ln(b)\)
10. Expand \(\log_5\!\left(\dfrac{x^{3}\,y}{z^{2}}\right)\)
Quotient: \(\log_5(x^3 y) - \log_5(z^2)\). Then product + power: \(3\log_5(x) + \log_5(y) - 2\log_5(z)\).
\(3\log_5(x) + \log_5(y) - 2\log_5(z)\)
11. Condense \(4\log(x) + 2\log(y) - 3\log(z)\)
Reverse power: \(\log(x^4) + \log(y^2) - \log(z^3) = \log\!\left(\dfrac{x^4 y^2}{z^3}\right)\).
\(\log\!\left(\dfrac{x^{4}\,y^{2}}{z^{3}}\right)\)
12. Condense \(\tfrac{1}{2}\ln(a) - 3\ln(b)\)
\(\ln(a^{1/2}) - \ln(b^3) = \ln\!\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{a}}{b^3}\right)\).
\(\ln\!\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{a}}{b^{3}}\right)\)
Part 2 — Log Equations
13. Solve \(\log_2(x) = 5\)
Domain: \(x > 0\).
Convert: \(x = 2^5 = 32\). \(32 > 0\) ✓.
\(x = 32\)
14. Solve \(\log_3(x-2) = 4\)
Domain: \(x > 2\).
\(x - 2 = 3^4 = 81 \Rightarrow x = 83\). ✓
\(x = 83\)
15. Solve \(\log_2(x) + \log_2(x+2) = 3\)
Domain: \(x > 0\).
\(\log_2(x(x+2)) = 3 \Rightarrow x(x+2) = 8\).
\(x^2 + 2x - 8 = 0 \Rightarrow (x+4)(x-2) = 0\).
\(x = 2\) or \(x = -4\). Reject \(-4\) (outside domain).
\(x = 2\)
16. Solve \(\log_5(x+1) - \log_5(x-3) = 1\)
Domain: \(x + 1 > 0\) AND \(x - 3 > 0 \Rightarrow x > 3\).
\(\log_5\!\left(\dfrac{x+1}{x-3}\right) = 1 \Rightarrow \dfrac{x+1}{x-3} = 5\).
\(x + 1 = 5(x-3) = 5x - 15 \Rightarrow -4x = -16 \Rightarrow x = 4\). \(4 > 3\) ✓.
\(x = 4\)
Part 3 — Exponential Modeling
17. Solve \(5^{x} = 14\)
\(x = \log_5(14) = \dfrac{\ln 14}{\ln 5} = \dfrac{2.6391}{1.6094} \approx 1.6397\).
\(x \approx 1.6397\)
18. \(\$3{,}000\) at \(5.5\%\) continuous \(\to\) \(\$9{,}000\)?
\(9000 = 3000\,e^{0.055\,t} \Rightarrow 3 = e^{0.055\,t}\).
\(\ln 3 = 0.055\,t \Rightarrow t = \dfrac{\ln 3}{0.055} = \dfrac{1.0986}{0.055} \approx 19.975\).
\(t \approx 19.97\) years
19. \(f(t) = 800(1.045)^{t} \to 800\,e^{rt}\)
(a) \((1.045)^t = e^{t\ln(1.045)} \Rightarrow r = \ln(1.045)\).
(b) Decimal: \(r \approx 0.04402\), so continuous rate \(\approx 4.40\%\) (slightly less than the discrete \(4.5\%\)).
(a) \(r = \ln(1.045)\)   (b) \(\approx 4.40\%\)
20. 100 g \(\to\) 10 g over 50 yr
(a) \(10 = 100\,e^{50k} \Rightarrow 0.1 = e^{50k} \Rightarrow k = \dfrac{\ln(0.1)}{50} = -\dfrac{\ln 10}{50} \approx -0.04605\).
(b) Half-life: \(\tfrac{1}{2} = e^{k\,t_h} \Rightarrow t_h = \dfrac{\ln(1/2)}{k} = \dfrac{-\ln 2}{-\ln 10/50} = \dfrac{50 \ln 2}{\ln 10} \approx \dfrac{50(0.6931)}{2.3026} \approx 15.05\).
(a) \(k = -\tfrac{\ln 10}{50}\)   (b) \(t_h \approx 15.05\) years
Part 4 — Fundamental Theorem of Algebra
21. \((3-i)\) zero of \(P(x) = x^{4} - 9x^{3} + 30x^{2} - 42x + 20\)
Step 1. Conjugate \((3+i)\) is also a zero.
Step 2. Quadratic factor: \((x-(3-i))(x-(3+i)) = (x-3)^2 - i^2 = x^2 - 6x + 10\).
Step 3. Divide \(P(x)\) by \(x^2 - 6x + 10\) (long division): \[ \dfrac{x^{4} - 9x^{3} + 30x^{2} - 42x + 20}{x^{2} - 6x + 10} = x^{2} - 3x + 2. \]
Step 4. Factor: \(x^2 - 3x + 2 = (x-1)(x-2)\).
All zeros: \(\{1,\;2,\;3-i,\;3+i\}\).
\(P(x) = (x-1)(x-2)\bigl(x-(3-i)\bigr)\bigl(x-(3+i)\bigr)\)
22. Degree 4, real int coefs, zeros \(x=2\) (mult 2) & \(x = 2+i\)
Step 1. Conjugate \(2-i\) is also a zero. Four zeros: \(2, 2, 2+i, 2-i\).
Step 2. Complex pair: \(\bigl(x-(2+i)\bigr)\bigl(x-(2-i)\bigr) = (x-2)^2 - i^2 = x^2 - 4x + 5\).
Step 3. \((x-2)^2 = x^2 - 4x + 4\). Multiply with \(x^2 - 4x + 5\): \[ (x^2 - 4x + 4)(x^2 - 4x + 5). \] Let \(u = x^2 - 4x\). Then \((u+4)(u+5) = u^2 + 9u + 20\). So: \[ (x^2 - 4x)^2 + 9(x^2 - 4x) + 20 = x^4 - 8x^3 + 16x^2 + 9x^2 - 36x + 20 = x^4 - 8x^3 + 25x^2 - 36x + 20. \]
\(P(x) = x^{4} - 8x^{3} + 25x^{2} - 36x + 20\)
23. Degree 3, real coefs, zeros \(x = -1\) and \(x = 3i\)
Conjugate \(-3i\) also a zero. \((x-3i)(x+3i) = x^2 + 9\).
\(P(x) = (x+1)(x^2 + 9) = x^3 + 9x + x^2 + 9 = x^3 + x^2 + 9x + 9\).
\(P(x) = x^{3} + x^{2} + 9x + 9\)
Part 5 — Synthetic Division & Factor Theorem
24. \((2x^{3} + 3x^{2} - 8x + 3) \div (x - 1)\)
\(c = 1\). Synthetic:
\(\begin{array}{r|rrrr} 1 & 2 & 3 & -8 & 3 \\ & & 2 & 5 & -3 \\ \hline & 2 & 5 & -3 & \boxed{0} \end{array}\)
Quotient \(Q(x) = 2x^2 + 5x - 3\); remainder \(R = 0 \Rightarrow (x-1)\) IS a factor.
\(Q(x) = 2x^{2} + 5x - 3,\; R = 0\). \((x-1)\) is a factor.
25. \(P(-2)\) for \(P(x) = x^{4} - 3x^{2} + 2x - 5\)
Insert zero for \(x^3\). Synthetic with \(c = -2\):
\(\begin{array}{r|rrrrr} -2 & 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 & -5 \\ & & -2 & 4 & -2 & 0 \\ \hline & 1 & -2 & 1 & 0 & \boxed{-5} \end{array}\)
By Remainder Theorem, \(P(-2) = -5\). Check directly: \(16 - 0 - 12 - 4 - 5 = -5\). ✓
\(P(-2) = -5\)
26. Find \(k\) so \((x-3)\) is factor of \(P(x) = x^{3} - 2x^{2} + kx - 6\)
Factor Theorem: \(P(3) = 0\).
\(P(3) = 27 - 18 + 3k - 6 = 3 + 3k = 0 \Rightarrow k = -1\).
\(k = -1\)
27. Factor completely: \(P(x) = x^{3} - 7x + 6\) (\(x = 1\) is a zero)
Verify: \(P(1) = 1 - 7 + 6 = 0\). ✓
Synthetic with \(c = 1\) (insert 0 for \(x^2\)):
\(\begin{array}{r|rrrr} 1 & 1 & 0 & -7 & 6 \\ & & 1 & 1 & -6 \\ \hline & 1 & 1 & -6 & \boxed{0} \end{array}\)
Quotient: \(x^2 + x - 6 = (x+3)(x-2)\).
\(P(x) = (x-1)(x-2)(x+3)\)
Part 6 — Polynomial Graphing
28. \(P(x) = (x-1)(x+2)^{2}(x-3)\)
Degree: \(1 + 2 + 1 = 4\). Leading term \(+x^4\) \(\Rightarrow\) end behavior: \(\uparrow\!\uparrow\) (both ends to \(+\infty\)).
Zeros: \(x = 1\) (mult 1, cross); \(x = -2\) (mult 2, bounce); \(x = 3\) (mult 1, cross).
\(y\)-intercept: \(P(0) = (-1)(2)^{2}(-3) = (-1)(4)(-3) = 12\). Point \((0, 12)\).
Deg 4, \(\uparrow\!\uparrow\); zeros: 1 (cross), \(-2\) (bounce), 3 (cross); \(y\)-int 12
29. \(P(x) = -x^{3}(x-2)(x+4)^{2}\)
Degree: \(3 + 1 + 2 = 6\). Leading term: \(-x^3 \cdot x \cdot x^2 = -x^6\) \(\Rightarrow\) end behavior: \(\downarrow\!\downarrow\) (both to \(-\infty\)).
Zeros: \(x = 0\) (mult 3, slide / flat-cross); \(x = 2\) (mult 1, cross); \(x = -4\) (mult 2, bounce).
\(y\)-intercept: \(P(0) = 0\) (graph passes through origin via slide).
Deg 6, \(\downarrow\!\downarrow\); zeros: 0 (slide), 2 (cross), \(-4\) (bounce); \(y\)-int 0
30. Build degree-4 with zeros \(-2\) (cross), 0 (bounce), 3 (cross), neg leading coef
Multiplicities: \(-2\) cross \(\Rightarrow\) mult 1; \(0\) bounce \(\Rightarrow\) mult 2; \(3\) cross \(\Rightarrow\) mult 1. Total \(1+2+1 = 4\). ✓
Equation (one valid choice): \(P(x) = -x^{2}(x+2)(x-3)\).
End behavior: degree 4 even, leading \(-1 \Rightarrow \downarrow\!\downarrow\).
Sketch: Coming from \(-\infty\) on left, crosses up at \(x=-2\), peaks above axis, touches and bounces down off zero at \(x=0\), goes back up briefly, crosses down at \(x=3\), drops to \(-\infty\) on right.
(Other valid forms: any negative scalar multiple, e.g. \(-2x^2(x+2)(x-3)\) etc.)
\(P(x) = -x^{2}(x+2)(x-3)\), end \(\downarrow\!\downarrow\)
